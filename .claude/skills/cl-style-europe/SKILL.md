# European Cover Letter Style

This skill defines how to write cover letter content for European job applications. It covers structure, tone, and regional norms. Visual formatting (fonts, margins, layout) is controlled by the style's `cl-format.yaml` in this folder.

Read `candidate.md` before writing. Every claim must be traceable to that file. Read `research.md` from the application folder for company-specific details.

---

## Structure: 4 Paragraphs, 250-400 Words Total

### Paragraph 1 — Hook (3-4 sentences)

Open with genuine, specific enthusiasm for THIS company. Reference something concrete: their product, mission, recent news, tech blog, or engineering culture (pull from research.md).

If research.md is unavailable or was skipped, open by referencing a specific requirement or challenge from the job description instead of company details.

Name the exact position being applied for. Briefly state why the candidate is a strong fit (1 sentence).

**Example of a good opener:**
```
Your work on democratizing air cargo booking through technology resonates with my experience building data platforms that serve diverse external clients at Motive. I am writing to apply for the Senior Data Engineer position.
```

**Example of a bad opener:**
```
I am writing to express my interest in the Senior Data Engineer position at your esteemed organization.
```

The difference: the good one proves you researched the company. The bad one could be sent anywhere.

### Paragraph 2 — Why I'm a Good Fit (4-6 sentences)

Connect 2-3 specific achievements from candidate.md directly to the JD requirements. Use quantified metrics but frame them as narrative, not bullet points. Mirror the posting's language and priorities.

If the role mentions specific challenges (scaling, migration, cost optimization), directly address how the candidate has solved similar problems.

### Paragraph 3 — Why This Company (3-4 sentences)

Demonstrate genuine knowledge of the company from research.md. Reference their product, tech stack, industry, culture, or values. Explain why the candidate wants to work HERE specifically — not just any European company.

If research.md is unavailable or was skipped, focus on why this role and its responsibilities excite the candidate — reference specific JD requirements, the tech stack listed, or the type of problems the role solves.

Mention relocation readiness naturally in this paragraph.

**Good relocation statement:**
```
I am committed to relocating to Berlin and am available to start within four weeks of an offer.
```

**Bad relocation statement:**
```
It has always been my dream to live and work in Europe.
```

### Paragraph 4 — Close (2-3 sentences)

Express enthusiasm for discussing the role further. Thank them for their consideration. Warm but professional — no desperation.

## Addressing and Sign-off

Address to "Dear Hiring Team" unless a specific contact name is provided. If a recruiter name is given, use "Dear [First Name] [Last Name]."

Sign off with "Kind regards," followed by the candidate's full name.

---

## Tone Rules

Write in a professional, warm, confident tone. European hiring culture values humility and directness over self-promotion.

Never use:
- "I am passionate about"
- "I would be honored"
- "It would be a dream to"
- "rockstar," "guru," "ninja," "hustle"
- Any sycophantic or groveling language

Use implied confidence: "I built X that achieved Y" — not "I believe I could contribute to..."

Vary sentence structure. Avoid formulaic transitions. The letter should feel human-written.

Every sentence should be impossible to send to a different company without editing. If a sentence could apply to any company, rewrite it.

---

## Country-Specific Tone

### Germany
Be thorough and precise. Include specific metrics and technical details. Structure logically: introduction, evidence, conclusion. Mention specific shared technologies.

### Netherlands
Be direct and concise. Get to the point quickly. Results-oriented language — what did you achieve, not what you're "excited" about. Aim for the lower end of 250-400 words. Authenticity matters more than polish.

### Denmark / Sweden
Emphasize collaboration and teamwork. Use "we achieved" alongside individual contributions. Flat hierarchy awareness — don't over-emphasize seniority. Understated confidence rather than bold claims.

### Belgium
Note the posting's language (French/Dutch/English). If writing in English, acknowledge the multilingual environment. Slightly more formal than Netherlands.

---

## What NOT to Include

Do not mention visa requirements, sponsorship needs, or immigration logistics — this is handled during screening calls.

Do not mention salary expectations unless explicitly asked.

Do not include references or "references available upon request."

Do not rehash the entire CV — the cover letter is a narrative, not a summary.

---

## Critical Rules

**ATS-safe characters only.** No unicode symbols, arrows, special dashes. Standard ASCII only. Hyphens (-) for dashes.

**Factual integrity.** Never fabricate. Every claim must trace back to candidate.md.

**Length.** 250-400 words. 4 paragraphs. 1 page maximum when formatted. These are hard limits.

**ATS keywords.** Naturally weave in key technologies from the JD. Use exact tool names: "Apache Airflow" not "orchestration tool." Don't keyword-stuff — it should read naturally.
