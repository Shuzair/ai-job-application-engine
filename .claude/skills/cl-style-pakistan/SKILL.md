# Pakistan Cover Letter Style

This style covers cover letter writing rules for Pakistan job applications. Visual formatting is controlled by the style's `cl-format.yaml` in this folder.

Read `candidate.md` before writing. Every claim must be traceable to that file. Read `research.md` from the application folder for company details.

---

## Structure: 3–4 Paragraphs, 180–350 Words Total

Pakistan cover letter norms (per ilm.com.pk, pakjobbazar.com — Tier 2): under one page, concise, 3–4 paragraphs. ilm.com.pk recommends approximately 150 words; for experienced candidates with relevant achievements to reference, up to 300–350 words is appropriate.

A cover letter is expected when applying directly to a company. When applying via portals (Rozee.pk, Mustakbil.com), it is still recommended but less universal.

### Paragraph 1 — Opening (2–3 sentences)

State the exact position you are applying for. Mention where you found the vacancy (e.g., "I came across this opportunity on Rozee.pk" or "via LinkedIn"). Open with a brief, specific statement connecting your background to the role. Per ilm.com.pk (Tier 2): "mention the esteemed organization by name — this impresses the employer."

Do NOT open with a generic statement like "I am writing to apply for..." without immediately following with something specific.

### Paragraph 2 — Why I'm a Good Fit (3–5 sentences)

Connect 2–3 specific, quantified achievements from candidate.md to the key requirements from the JD. Lead with the most impressive, role-relevant metric. Use formal, confident language without being boastful. Do not simply restate the CV — demonstrate impact and relevance through concise narrative.

Example approach: "In my role at [Company], I [achievement with metric], which directly aligns with [specific JD requirement]."

### Paragraph 3 — Why This Company (2–3 sentences)

Demonstrate genuine knowledge of the company from research.md. Explain why the candidate wants to work for this specific organization (sector position, product, mission, growth, culture). Mention relocation readiness if the candidate is applying from a different city.

Avoid hollow flattery. Reference specific facts: the company's industry standing, a notable product or initiative, or a recent development.

### Paragraph 4 — Close (2–3 sentences)

Express willingness to discuss further. Thank the employer for their time and consideration. Reference the attached CV.

Example: "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience can contribute to [Company Name]. Please find my CV attached for your consideration. Thank you for your time."

## Addressing and Sign-off

**Salutation options (in order of preference):**
1. "Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name]," — when the hiring manager's name is known
2. "Dear Hiring Manager," — modern standard when name is unknown
3. "Respected Sir," / "Respected Ma'am," — formal Pakistani convention, appropriate for traditional industries, government, and NGOs
4. "Dear Sir/Madam," — acceptable formal fallback

Do not use "To Whom It May Concern" — it is considered dated by modern Pakistani recruiters.

**Sign-off (British Commonwealth convention — per ilm.com.pk, Tier 2):**
- "Yours sincerely," — when the recipient is named (Paragraph 1 addresses "Dear Mr. Khan")
- "Yours faithfully," — when the recipient is unnamed ("Dear Hiring Manager" / "Dear Sir/Madam")
- "Respectfully yours," — appropriate for government, academic, and NGO applications

Default: use "Yours sincerely," and address by name whenever possible.

## Tone Rules

- **Formal and professional throughout.** English is the universal standard for private sector, multinationals, and tech companies. Do not use Urdu.
- **Respectful deference**: Pakistan's business culture values hierarchy and formal respect (Cultural Atlas, Tier 3). The tone should convey confidence in qualifications without aggressive self-promotion.
- **Positive framing**: Avoid any negative phrasing (e.g., "I lack experience in X" or "Although I have not..."). Frame gaps as growth opportunities only if unavoidable.
- **Concise substance**: Every sentence must earn its place. Avoid padding. Pakistani recruiters value brevity.
- **Specific over generic**: Name the company, the role, and specific achievements. Generic phrases that could appear in any cover letter signal low effort.
- **Words and phrases to use**: "aligned with," "directly relevant to," "contributed to," "delivered," "achieved," "eager to contribute," "look forward to discussing"
- **Words and phrases to avoid**: "passionate," "hardworking" (overused), "I am the perfect candidate" (too aggressive), superlatives without evidence

## What NOT to Include

- Salary expectations (unless explicitly requested in the job posting)
- References or referee names
- CNIC number, religion, marital status, or date of birth
- Visa or immigration status for domestic applications
- Personal information beyond name, contact details in the header
- Any claim not traceable to candidate.md
- "Dear Sir/Madam, I am writing to apply" as the full opening sentence — too generic

## Critical Rules

- **ATS-safe characters**: Plain text, no special formatting or symbols.
- **Factual integrity**: Every claim traceable to candidate.md.
- **Word count**: Stay within 180–350 words. ilm.com.pk recommends ~150 words; the upper limit is firm — one page maximum.
- **Keywords**: Naturally integrate JD keywords without keyword stuffing. Mirror the JD's terminology.
- **Email subject line**: If submitting via email, the subject should include the job title and reference number (if provided). The cover letter body itself should still reference where the vacancy was found.
- **Date format in letter**: Use "29 May 2026" (DD Month YYYY) — British-style date format, placed at top-left per formal Pakistani business letter convention.
- **No "Curriculum Vitae" or "Resume" as a title**: The letter stands alone; reference it simply as "my CV" or "my attached application."
