# Lithuania Cover Letter Style

This style covers cover letter writing rules for Lithuania job applications. Visual formatting is controlled by the style's `cl-format.yaml` in this folder.

Read `candidate.md` before writing. Every claim must be traceable to that file. Read `research.md` from the application folder for company details.

**Document name in Lithuanian**: "Motyvacinis laiškas" (motivation letter) — this is the universally used Lithuanian term.

**Language**: Write in English for international companies (fintech, IT, shared services). Write in Lithuanian only if the posting explicitly requires it or the employer is a local Lithuanian company.

**Cover letter is expected**: Per the Lithuanian Employment Service, submitting both a CV and a cover letter is the standard application process. It is not optional for professional roles.

---

## Structure: 4 Paragraphs, 250–400 Words Total

A Lithuanian motyvacinis laiškas is concise and structured. One A4 page maximum. 3–4 paragraphs. Each paragraph has a clear purpose.

### Paragraph 1 — Introduction (3–4 sentences)

Open with a specific, genuine reason for applying to this company. Reference something concrete from research.md — a product, a market position, a recent achievement, or a stated company value that resonates. Name the exact position you're applying for.

Do not open with "I am writing to apply for…" — that is a generic opener Lithuanian recruiters see constantly. Start with what drew you to this company specifically.

### Paragraph 2 — Why I'm a Good Fit (4–6 sentences)

Connect 2–3 specific achievements from candidate.md to the role's requirements. Lead with quantified results — Lithuanian employers value measurable, evidence-backed claims over qualitative descriptions. Expand briefly on 1–2 metrics that appear in the CV to give context and narrative.

Use JD terminology directly. If the posting says "data infrastructure," use that phrase — not a synonym.

Avoid: "I am passionate about…", "I am highly motivated…", or any superlative-based self-description. Let the numbers do the work.

### Paragraph 3 — Why This Company (3–4 sentences)

Demonstrate specific knowledge of the company from research.md. Explain why this company and this role fit the candidate's trajectory — not just generic enthusiasm. Connect one aspect of the company's work (product, market, culture, tech stack) to the candidate's background or goals.

If applying from abroad: mention relocation readiness here. Lithuanian employers appreciate directness about availability and logistics.

### Paragraph 4 — Close (2–3 sentences)

Express genuine interest in discussing the role further. Thank them for their consideration. Keep it brief and direct — Lithuanians appreciate purposeful communication without excessive formality.

## Addressing and Sign-off

**Addressing**:
- If the hiring manager's name is known: "Dear Mr./Ms. [Surname]," — use formal title and surname only
- Lithuanian formal equivalent: "Gerbiamas Ponas [Surname]," (Mr.) or "Gerbiama Ponia [Surname]," (Ms.)
- If name is unknown: "Dear Hiring Team," or the Lithuanian "Gerbiami,"
- Do not use first names unless explicitly invited to do so
- Do not use "To Whom It May Concern" — it signals no research was done

**Sign-off**:
- Lithuanian: "Pagarbiai," — this is the universally used professional closing (equivalent to "Respectfully" or "Sincerely")
- English alternative: "Kind regards," or "Yours sincerely,"
- Use "Pagarbiai," for letters in Lithuanian; "Kind regards," for letters in English to international employers
- Follow with the candidate's full name

## Tone Rules

**Formal but warm.** Lithuanian workplace communication sits between formal British correspondence and warm Nordic directness — structured and professional, but genuine. Not cold, not effusive.

**Direct and evidence-driven.** Lithuanians value pragmatism. Every claim should be backed by a fact or metric. "I increased pipeline throughput by 40%" lands better than "I am a driven professional with a passion for data."

**Modest confidence.** Avoid boastful language. Never say you are "the best candidate" or use comparative superlatives. Present facts and let the reader draw the conclusion.

**No personal pronouns overload.** "I" is acceptable in cover letters (unlike CV bullets), but vary sentence structure. Don't start every sentence with "I."

**Avoid these phrases entirely**:
- "I am passionate about…"
- "I am a highly motivated self-starter…"
- "I would be a great asset to your team"
- "I believe I am the perfect fit…"
- Generic openers: "I am writing to apply for…"

**Use instead**:
- Lead with company-specific observation or shared value
- Use action-oriented language referencing past results
- Frame fit as a logical match between candidate's track record and the role's requirements

---

## What NOT to Include

- Visa or sponsorship status in the body — relocation readiness is fine, but visa mechanics are not
- Salary expectations — never mention unless the posting explicitly asks for them
- References — these belong in the CV's Rekomendacijos section
- Personal/private life details (protected by Lithuania's Law on Equal Treatment, 2003)
- Date of birth, marital status, or family plans
- Photo — cover letters never include photos
- Repetition of the CV — the letter must add narrative context, not summarize the CV

---

## Critical Rules

- **One A4 page maximum**: 250–400 words. Lithuanian recruiters expect brevity. Over 400 words signals poor editing.
- **ATS-safe characters**: Plain text, no special formatting.
- **Factual integrity**: Every claim traceable to candidate.md.
- **Keywords**: Naturally integrate JD keywords — mirror the JD's terminology in paragraph 2. Do not keyword-stuff.
- **Company specificity**: Generic letters that could apply to any company are ineffective in Lithuania. At least one specific company detail from research.md must appear.
- **Anti-discrimination compliance**: Do not include personal details that employers are legally prohibited from requesting under Lithuania's Law on Equal Treatment (2003) — age, family status, personal life details.
