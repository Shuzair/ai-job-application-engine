# Qatar Cover Letter Style

This style covers cover letter writing rules for Qatar job applications. Visual formatting is controlled by the style's `cl-format.yaml` in this folder.

Read `candidate.md` before writing. Every claim must be traceable to that file. Read `research.md` from the application folder for company details.

---

## Structure: 3–4 Paragraphs, 250–400 Words Total

Cover letters in Qatar are formal, one-page business documents. English is the primary language for private sector, multinational, and QFC-registered company applications. An Arabic version may be appropriate or required for government, semi-government, or purely Qatari-owned entities — when in doubt, default to English.

Include a cover letter whenever the application provides an attachment field or free-text area. Gulf employers consider a well-crafted cover letter a meaningful differentiator, though many online applications via Bayt.com or Naukrigulf accept CV-only submissions.

### Paragraph 1 — Opening (3–4 sentences)

State the position applied for clearly and early. Open with genuine, specific enthusiasm for the company and role. Reference something concrete from research.md — a recent project, the company's sector positioning, a major initiative, or their role in Qatar's national development. Avoid generic openers ("I am writing to apply for…" without further substance).

If the candidate was referred to this opportunity by a professional contact at the organisation, mention this here: "I was referred to this opportunity by [Name], [Title] at your organisation." Referrals carry significant weight in Qatar's relationship-driven hiring culture.

### Paragraph 2 — Why I'm a Good Fit (4–6 sentences)

Connect 2–3 specific achievements from candidate.md to the JD requirements. Use quantified metrics as narrative — team size, contract value, percentage improvements, project duration, budget managed. Mirror exact terminology from the job description, including sector-specific language (EPC contracts, IFRS compliance, HSE management, LNG operations, QA/QC, etc.).

For expat candidates: briefly highlight regional adaptability or prior GCC/Qatar experience if available. Framing contribution in terms of knowledge transfer and capacity building resonates with Qatarization workforce development goals and is viewed favourably.

### Paragraph 3 — Why This Company (3–4 sentences)

Demonstrate genuine knowledge of the organisation from research.md. Explain why the candidate wants to work for this specific company. For Qatar, reference: industry leadership, Qatar National Vision 2030 alignment, the organisation's Qatarization programme, major ongoing projects, or growth initiatives. Avoid generic praise.

If applying from abroad: restate willingness to relocate to Qatar and confirm QID/visa status briefly if it strengthens the application.

### Paragraph 4 — Close (2–3 sentences)

Express enthusiasm for discussing the role further. Confirm availability for interview. Thank them for their time and consideration. Keep this brief and professional — do not repeat earlier content.

## Addressing and Sign-off

**Addressing:**
- Named recipient: "Dear Mr./Ms. [Surname]," — formal title + surname is standard
- Unknown recipient: "Dear Sir/Madam," or "Dear Hiring Manager," — both acceptable. Never use "To Whom It May Concern" — explicitly discouraged in Qatar hiring norms (ResumeFlex)
- Qatari-owned organisations / government entities: "Assalamu alaikum," followed by the recipient's full name and title is culturally appropriate and positively received
- Formal / government applications: "Dear Hiring Committee,"

**Sign-off:**
- Named recipient: "Yours sincerely,"
- Unnamed recipient: "Yours faithfully,"
- Multinational / international company: "Best regards," or "Respectfully yours,"
- Arabic-addressed letter: "مع خالص التحية والتقدير" (With sincere regards and respect)

Follow the sign-off with the candidate's full name on the next line. Optionally include the candidate's title and contact details in the sign-off block if not already in the header.

## Tone Rules

Formal and respectful. Qatar's business culture values modesty and cultural sensitivity — the tone should combine achievement with measured, deferential phrasing. Avoid hyperbolic self-promotion ("I am the ideal candidate," "world-class expertise," "unmatched track record").

- Warm but not casual; professional distance is maintained throughout
- Achievement-oriented but appropriately modest (e.g., "I had the privilege of leading…" rather than "I single-handedly delivered…")
- Highlight commitment to the organisation's mission and, where natural, to Qatar's development goals
- British English spelling throughout: "organisation," "specialise," "recognise," "programme," "favour"
- Do not start sentences with "I" — restructure as needed to avoid it
- No personal information beyond what is in the header

---

## What NOT to Include

- Salary expectations (unless explicitly requested by the employer)
- References (handled in the CV's references section)
- Visa or sponsorship requests framed as a burden — instead state availability positively ("Currently holding a valid QID with NOC" or "Open to immediate relocation to Qatar")
- Tribal, family, or religious affiliations
- Generic phrases unrelated to this specific company and role
- Content that duplicates CV bullet points verbatim — the cover letter adds narrative context, not a repeat of achievements already listed

---

## Critical Rules

- **One page maximum**: Expatica explicitly states Qatar cover letters must not exceed one page. Enforce the 250–400 word total strictly.
- **ATS-safe characters**: Plain text only — no special formatting, tables, or decorative elements.
- **Factual integrity**: Every claim must be traceable to candidate.md.
- **British English**: "organisation," "specialise," "optimise," "programme," "recognise."
- **Referral mention**: If a wasta/professional referral connection exists, name the contact in Paragraph 1 — this is culturally expected and signals insider validation in Qatar's relationship-driven hiring environment.
- **No "To Whom It May Concern"**: Explicitly discouraged in Qatar job application norms. Use "Dear Sir/Madam" or "Dear Hiring Manager" instead.
- **Qatarization framing**: For expat candidates applying to organisations with Qatarization quotas, naturally emphasise skills that complement (rather than compete with) Qatari workforce development — knowledge transfer, training local teams, capacity building.
