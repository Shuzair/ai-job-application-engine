# Dubai Cover Letter Style

This style covers cover letter writing rules for Dubai/UAE job applications. Visual formatting is controlled by `cl-format.yaml` in this folder.

Read `candidate.md` before writing. Every claim must be traceable to that file. Read `research.md` from the application folder for company details.

---

## Structure: 4 Paragraphs, 200–350 Words Total

Four-paragraph framework per Labeeb.ae's UAE-specific cover letter guidance. One page maximum, regardless of seniority. Concise and achievement-dense — UAE recruiters prefer letters that get to quantified outcomes fast.

Seniority guidance:
- Graduate/entry-level: 180–220 words
- Mid-career (4–10 years): 250–320 words
- Senior/executive: 300–350 words

### Paragraph 1 — Opening Hook (2-3 sentences)

State the exact role title and reference number (if provided). Immediately signal visa status and notice period/earliest joining date — this is functionally required in UAE cover letters so the recruiter does not need to hunt for it. Add one specific detail about the company from research.md that shows genuine knowledge (not generic praise).

Format: "I am applying for the [Role Title] position [ref: XXX if available]. I hold an [Employment/Visit/Golden] Visa and am available [immediately / in X days / from [date]]. [One specific, researched company detail that explains why this company, not just any company in the sector]."

Per Labeeb.ae: opening with visa status and availability is the #1 way UAE cover letters differ from Western templates — do not bury this information.

### Paragraph 2 — Quantified Fit (3-4 sentences)

Connect 2-3 specific achievements from candidate.md directly to JD requirements. Lead with AED-denominated or %-quantified outcomes. Use exact terminology from the JD. This is the core value paragraph — treat it as a mini-pitch of the three most relevant data points.

Per Labeeb.ae, 2-3 measurable outcomes expressed in AED or percentages are the strongest signal in a UAE cover letter. Generic claims without metrics are a recruiter red flag.

### Paragraph 3 — UAE Market Fit (2-3 sentences)

Signal UAE/GCC market relevance. Include one or more of:
- Relevant UAE regulatory awareness (DIFC, ADGM, RERA, CBUAE, DHA, KHDA, AML-CFT, UAE VAT — only if genuine and role-relevant)
- GCC regional experience or cross-regional project scope
- Multicultural team experience (emphasizing the international nature of Dubai's workforce)
- UAE Vision 2031 / D33 agenda alignment (only if the company's mission or JD signals this context)
- Arabic language capability if relevant

Do not manufacture UAE relevance. If the candidate has no GCC experience, focus on the transferable global skills and express genuine motivation to build UAE market knowledge.

### Paragraph 4 — Confident Close (1-2 sentences)

Express genuine enthusiasm for the role and the company. End with a direct, confident call to action: "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss [specific aspect] further."

Per Labeeb.ae, avoid apologetic closings such as "Thank you for considering my application" — these signal subordinate positioning. A confident, professional close is expected.

## Addressing and Sign-off

**Salutation**: "Dear Ms. [Surname]" or "Dear Mr. [Surname]" when a contact name is findable from the job posting or research.md. Acceptable fallback: "Dear Talent Acquisition Team" or "Dear Hiring Manager." Avoid "To Whom It May Concern" — per Labeeb.ae, this is explicitly flagged as a recruiter red flag in the UAE market.

For Arabic-language letters to federal government entities (rare for expatriate applicants): الفاضل [Name] or السلام عليكم ورحمة الله وبركاته as opening.

**Sign-off**:
- Standard English: "Kind regards," or "Sincerely," followed by full name
- For DIFC/ADGM financial services: "Yours sincerely," (banking-grade formality)
- Avoid: "Warm regards," (too casual for traditional UAE companies), "Cheers," (inappropriate)
- For government or federal entity submissions (PDF format): a typed name block is standard; a scanned handwritten signature adds formality if the application context is formal

## Tone Rules

**English is standard** for all private sector, MNC, free zone, and most mid-size company applications. Arabic-language cover letters are required or strongly preferred only for: federal government roles, Abu Dhabi public entities (ADNOC, Mubadala, ADIB), and roles where Arabic is explicitly listed as a requirement.

**Tone varies by sector**:
- Finance/banking (DIFC/ADGM): banking-grade precision, formal language, no colloquialisms
- Tech startups and free zones: direct, pragmatic, outcome-focused, slightly warmer
- Government/federal entities: governance-oriented, Vision 2031 alignment language, formal
- General private sector: professional and direct, as in a senior meeting — not cold, not overly warm

**Phrases to use**:
- "GCC regional experience"
- "UAE market context"
- "Multicultural team environment"
- "[AED-denominated metric]"
- Role-relevant zone or regulator names (DIFC, RERA, DHA, etc.)

**Phrases to avoid**:
- "Passionate about" — overused filler
- "Results-driven" — cliché, says nothing
- "Dynamic team player" — ATS noise
- "Esteemed organisation" — sycophantic generic praise
- "Highly respected company" — same
- "Thank you for considering my application" as a close — signals subordinate positioning
- Any mention of personal connections or referrals (wasta references are inappropriate in a formal cover letter)

## What NOT to Include

- **Passport numbers or Emirates ID** — explicitly listed by Labeeb.ae as information to exclude from cover letters
- **Salary expectations** — unless the job posting explicitly requests them
- **References** — "Available upon request" is the norm; do not list names
- **Photos in the letter body** — photos belong only on the CV
- **Religion** — not appropriate in a cover letter
- **Marital status** — belongs in the CV header for traditional companies if at all, not in the cover letter
- **Repetition of CV content** — no narrative job history recap. The letter adds context; it does not summarise the CV
- **Multi-column design or decorative fonts** — plain single-column, ATS-safe format only
- **Generic praise without specifics** — every company-specific statement must be backed by a concrete detail from research.md

## Critical Rules

- **ATS-safe**: Plain text, standard characters, single-column layout. UAE employers use ATS to scan cover letters in addition to CVs.
- **Factual integrity**: Every claim traceable to candidate.md. Every company reference traceable to research.md.
- **Word count**: Enforce the total word limit strictly. UAE recruiters read dozens of letters — brevity signals respect for their time.
- **Visa/availability in opening**: This is the top structural difference from Western cover letters. Must appear in Paragraph 1.
- **AED-denominated metrics**: Use AED for financial metrics where authentic — signals UAE market familiarity.
- **One page maximum**: No exceptions. If content exceeds one page at the prescribed margins, cut the least impactful sentences.
- **Keywords**: Naturally integrate JD terminology — especially UAE-specific zone/regulator names, regulatory frameworks, and product/platform names from the JD.
