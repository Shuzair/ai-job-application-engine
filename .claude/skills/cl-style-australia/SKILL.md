# Australia Cover Letter Style

This style covers cover letter writing rules for Australia job applications. Visual formatting is controlled by the style's `cl-format.yaml` in this folder.

Read `candidate.md` before writing. Every claim must be traceable to that file. Read `research.md` from the application folder for company details.

Use **Australian English** throughout: organisation, colour, programme, labour, analyse, recognised. American spelling is a visible red flag to Australian recruiters.

---

## Structure: 4 Paragraphs, 300–450 Words Total

The standard private-sector Australian cover letter is one page, 300–450 words, 4 paragraphs. A cover letter is not always explicitly required, but is strongly recommended — it is widely cited as "the difference between a quick shortlist and a quiet rejection" (Hays Australia). Only omit when the application system has no cover letter field.

Note: Government/public sector applications use a different format (Statement Addressing Selection Criteria using STAR methodology). This skill covers private sector only.

### Paragraph 1 — Opening Hook (3–4 sentences)

State the exact position title and where you saw it advertised (if known). Open with a specific, genuine observation about the company — drawn from research.md (recent news, product, mission, culture signal). This immediately demonstrates you have done your homework and are not sending a generic letter.

Avoid generic openers: "I am writing to express my interest in…" leads with nothing. Instead, lead with insight or a direct value statement.

**Good opener:** "The way [Company] is scaling its real-time data infrastructure to support [X users/product] is exactly the kind of challenge I have spent the last five years building solutions for — which is why I am excited to apply for the Senior Data Engineer role."

**Bad opener:** "I am a highly motivated and dynamic professional who is passionate about innovation."

### Paragraph 2 — Why I'm a Good Fit (4–6 sentences)

Connect 2–3 specific achievements from candidate.md to the requirements from the JD. Use quantified metrics — the numbers do the talking, consistent with Australian norms around not overselling. Structure as: here is what I did, here is the evidence, here is how it maps to what you need.

Do not list everything — select the 2–3 achievements most directly relevant to this specific role and company. Quality over quantity.

### Paragraph 3 — Why This Company (3–4 sentences)

Demonstrate genuine knowledge of the company using research.md. Reference something specific — a product, a mission, a recent development, a values statement. Explain why the candidate wants to work there specifically, not just why they want any role in the industry.

If the candidate is relocating interstate or from overseas, briefly mention availability and relocation readiness here or in the closing paragraph.

### Paragraph 4 — Close (2–3 sentences)

Express enthusiasm for discussing the role further. Thank them for their consideration. State availability for interview (and if relevant, note notice period — Hays Australia specifically recommends including this). Brief and confident, not desperate.

**Example close:** "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience can contribute to [Company]'s goals. Thank you for your consideration. I am available for an interview at your convenience and can start within [X weeks] of accepting an offer."

## Addressing and Sign-off

**Salutation:**
- If the hiring manager's name is known: "Dear [First Name Last Name]," — Australian business culture, especially in tech, creative, and marketing, often uses first name for less formal sectors. For finance, law, and senior roles: "Dear [Last Name],"
- If name is unknown: "Dear Hiring Manager," (strongly preferred over "To Whom It May Concern" — considered outdated — or "Dear Sir/Madam" — gender-specific and dated)

**Date format:** Left-aligned at the top of the letter (full block format). Use: "2 June 2026" (day month year, no ordinal suffix — the standard Australian business letter format).

**Sign-off — Australian standard closings in order of frequency:**
1. "Kind regards," — most commonly recommended by Hays, Michael Page, SEEK
2. "Regards," — acceptable and common
3. "Warm regards," — warmer tone, appropriate for less formal sectors (tech, startup)
4. "Sincerely," — formally correct, slightly US-influenced but widely used

Follow sign-off with: full typed name, phone number, email address (not name alone).

## Tone Rules

**Aim for:** Professional, warm, direct, and evidence-led. The Australian cover letter sits between the stiff formality of UK letters and the overt enthusiasm of US letters. Clear and concise sentences. Use "I" and "you" naturally. Plain Australian English; avoid jargon unless industry-specific.

**Tall poppy caution:** The Australian cultural norm of not overselling. Superlatives ("exceptional," "world-class," "top-performing") read as boastful. Replace with evidence: instead of "I am an exceptional data engineer," write "I reduced pipeline latency by 60%."

**Avoid clichés:** "Dynamic team player," "passionate about excellence," "highly motivated," "results-driven" — Australian recruiters specifically flag these as hollow filler. Each sentence must earn its place with a concrete detail.

**Active language:** Write in first person and active voice. "I led the migration" not "the migration was led by me."

**Length discipline:** 300–450 words is non-negotiable for private sector. Edit ruthlessly. Anything over one page will not be read.

## What NOT to Include

- Photo (legally and culturally inappropriate)
- Date of birth, age, nationality (discrimination law applies)
- Marital status, religion, gender
- Salary expectations (discuss in interview or via application system)
- References or referee details in the cover letter
- Generic statements about wanting career growth without linking to this specific company
- Selection Criteria responses — this is for government applications only; a private-sector letter that reads like an APS Statement Addressing Criteria is immediately out of place
- Work rights/visa status: If relevant, include a brief, confident statement only: "I hold a valid Subclass 482 visa with full working rights." Do not over-explain or apologise.

## Critical Rules

- **ATS-safe characters**: Plain text, no special formatting.
- **Factual integrity**: Every claim traceable to candidate.md.
- **Word count**: Enforce 300–450 words strictly. Trim the closing paragraph first if needed.
- **Australian English**: Every word. "Organisation," "colour," "programme," "recognise," "finalise."
- **Keywords**: Naturally integrate 2–3 JD keywords without stuffing. The letter must read as a human wrote it.
- **Specificity**: Every paragraph must contain at least one specific, concrete detail (company name, product, metric, JD term). Generic sentences are a disqualifying signal in Australian applications.
- **No Key Selection Criteria format**: This style is for private sector only. Do not use STAR-method headers or criteria numbering in a private sector cover letter.
